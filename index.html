Computer architecture, spatial accelerators, and hardware/software systems.

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at William & Mary. My research interests center on computer architecture, spatial/data-flow accelerators, hardware/software co-design, and performance evaluation for emerging accelerator systems.

Before joining William & Mary, I received my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Georgia Tech and my B.S. in Computer Engineering from Virginia Tech. I also worked as a DFT engineer, where I contributed to FPGA SoC testing infrastructure, scan-chain insertion, ATPG optimization, and simulation flows for pre-silicon and post-silicon validation.

Research Interests

Current directions

Spatial/Data-flow Accelerators

Execution models and architectural support for CGRAs, systolic arrays, and other spatial computing systems.

Simulation & Evaluation

Cycle-level simulation, validation methodology, and design-space exploration for accelerator architectures.

Compiler–Architecture Interface

Mapping artifacts and intermediate representations that connect compiler decisions with backend execution behavior.
